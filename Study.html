Joule's Heating Law

Joule's Heating Law states that the heat produced in a conductor is proportional to the square of the current, the resistance of the conductor, and the time for which the current flows. It is represented as H = I²RT.

This law is critical for understanding electrical circuits, electric heaters, and other applications where heat generation from current flow is important.

Practice Problems

  • If a current of 2A flows through a 5Ω resistor for 10s, calculate the heat produced.
  • A 12V battery is connected across a resistor. If the current flowing is 1.5A, and it flows for 5 minutes, find the heat generated.
